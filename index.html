Breathable Waterproof Membrane

The Barrier: This advanced membrane is what makes AeroStride work in all weather. It allows air to flow while blocking water—a feat of material science.

ePTFE membrane specifications:

  • Thickness: 0.5 millimeters
  • Pore size: 0.2 micrometers
  • Pore density: 600 million per square centimeter
  • Water column: 3000mm (waterproof rating)
  • Breathability: 5000+ g/m²/24hr

Technology: The same technology NASA uses in spacesuits and mountaineers use in extreme weather gear. Proven reliability across 40+ years of use.

Silicon Cooling Pads

The Heat Sink: These specially engineered pads absorb and dissipate the heat generated by friction and metabolic activity during running.

Pad specifications:

  • Thermal conductivity: 1.5 W/m·K
  • Heat absorption capacity: 850 J/g·K
  • Coverage area: 45% of insole surface
  • Thickness: 4mm optimized for quick response
  • Durability: Non-degrading over 5+ years

Placement: Positioned at the ball of foot, heel, and arch—the three main heat generation zones during running.

Air Exhaust System

The Exit Strategy: After cooling your foot and absorbing heat, warm air must exit efficiently. Our exhaust system ensures continuous flow without creating pressure buildup.

Exhaust design:

  • Primary exhaust ports: 4 at toe box edges
  • Secondary exhaust: 2 at heel counter
  • Pressure relief: Automatic opening at 120+ millibars
  • Non-blocking: Design prevents moisture re-entry

Result: Continuous airflow with zero moisture backdraft. Your feet stay both cool and dry.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does AeroStride work without batteries or electricity?

The TPU pump in the heel and midsole compresses with every footstep, generating 50-150 millibars of pressure. This pressure drives fresh air through the filtration system, cooling channels, and out through exhaust vents. It's pure mechanical energy conversion—no electronics required. The faster you run, the more active the cooling system becomes.

Does it need batteries?

Absolutely not. Zero batteries, zero charging cables, zero electronic maintenance. The shoe is entirely mechanical. You could wear it for 10 years and the cooling system would work exactly as well as day one. This is one of our core advantages over electronic alternatives.

Is it waterproof?

Yes. The breathable waterproof ePTFE membrane (rated at 3000mm water column) allows air to pass while blocking water. You can run in rain, wade through puddles, and hike in wet grass without worrying about water entering the cooling system. The membrane is proven technology used in spacesuits and extreme weather gear.

What is ePTFE and why does it matter?

ePTFE (Expanded PTFE) is an advanced material consisting of millions of tiny pores (0.2 micrometers) per square centimeter. These pores are small enough to block water molecules (which are larger) but large enough to allow air molecules through. It's the material used in high-end Gore-Tex products and NASA spacesuits. In AeroStride, it's the key to our waterproof-yet-breathable design.

Who is AeroStride designed for?

Three main audiences: (1) Serious runners and athletes who spend hours in their shoes; (2) Professionals who stand for long periods (retail workers, surgeons, etc.); (3) Anyone who wants better comfort and doesn't want to compromise on performance. We have Standard for everyday use and Elite for extreme conditions.

What makes Elite different from Standard?

Elite includes dual TPU pumps (heel + midsole), extended air channel network, enhanced cooling pads, advanced three-stage filtration, and a carbon reinforcement plate. Standard uses single-chamber pump with standard ventilation. Elite is designed for marathoners and professionals; Standard is perfect for casual runners.

How long will AeroStride last?

The mechanical system lasts indefinitely—we're not aware of any lifespan limits on TPU pumps under normal use. The midsole foam will eventually compress (typical for any shoe), but the cooling system never wears out. We typically recommend replacement after 300-500 miles of running, same as any quality athletic shoe.

How do I maintain the dust filter?

Very simple. Once every 20-30 days of use, remove the filter by gently prying from the side of the shoe, rinse under water, and air dry completely. It's designed to be washable and reusable indefinitely. No special chemicals or tools needed.

Will it work when I walk slowly or stand still?

Walking generates less pressure than running, so cooling is reduced during casual walking. When standing completely still, the system doesn't operate—but that's okay because your feet aren't generating heat through activity. The shoe excels during active use: running, hiking, playing sports, or any movement that keeps you active.

Can I wear these in extreme heat?

Absolutely. In fact, AeroStride shines in hot conditions because the active cooling system generates continuous airflow. Testing in 45°C+ temperatures showed 6-8°C reduction compared to traditional shoes. This is exactly the scenario Elite was designed for.

Are there size options?
